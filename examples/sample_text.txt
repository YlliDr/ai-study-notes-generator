Natural Language Processing is a branch of artificial intelligence that focuses on helping computers understand and generate human language. NLP includes tasks such as text classification, sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, machine translation, summarization, and text generation. Older NLP systems used rule-based methods and traditional machine learning algorithms. Modern NLP systems often use neural networks and transformer architectures. Transformers use attention mechanisms to understand relationships between words and context. Pretrained transformer models can be reused for many tasks through transfer learning, which makes them useful even when limited training data is available.
